What is the real problem?
Market women lose 2 to 3 hours daily waiting in vendor queues because there is no system to signal when popular items will be restocked. This forces buyers to arrive early and wait blindly rather than time their arrival to actual supply. The problem is not that queues exist — it is that no information flows between the people who control supply and the people who need it.
Who is affected? (3 groups)
Market Women

Lose income-generating time and suffer physical exhaustion waiting in sun or rain. Time in queues cannot be used for trading, childcare, or other productive activity.

Children

Left unsupervised at home or brought into crowded, unsafe environments. Younger children face health risks; older children may miss school.

Vendors

Face chaotic customer surges the moment stock arrives, making it difficult to serve customers efficiently, maintain order, or plan staffing.

One assumption engineers might make that is WRONG
That buyers have smartphones and reliable mobile data, so an app-based notification system will solve the problem. In reality, most market women in Oshodi or Makola use basic feature phones, have inconsistent airtime, and cannot afford mobile data bundles. Any solution must function via SMS or physical signals — not apps — to be genuinely inclusive.
