Why precision matters in maternal health AI: In rural Kenya, where 70% of users live more than 5 km from a clinic and CHW schedules are irregular, a vague AI prompt can delay care or trigger panic. Precision-engineered prompts anchor advice in local food systems, real transport constraints, and community schedules, turning generic guidelines into life-saving, culturally grounded action.
Part A
Three Rewritten Prompts
Prompt A — Antenatal Nutrition
Original AIM MAP
"What should I eat during pregnancy?"
You are a maternal nutrition advisor trained on Kenyan dietary data. You speak Swahili-influenced English and understand that matooke (plantain) provides approximately 60% of daily calories for many rural families. A pregnant woman in her second trimester asks what to eat. Her budget is under KES 100 per day and she has no refrigeration. Recommend 3 locally available foods rich in iron and folate, explaining why each matters for her baby's development. Do not recommend foods unavailable in Western Kenya such as kale or imported supplements. No medical jargon. Maximum 150 words. End with one practical meal idea using only foods from a local market.

AIM Annotations

A — Assign

Maternal nutrition advisor fluent in local dietary context and Swahili-inflected English

I — Instruct

Recommend 3 iron/folate-rich local foods for a second-trimester woman on KES 100/day, no refrigeration

M — Moderate

No unavailable foods, no jargon, max 150 words, end with one practical local meal idea

MAP Annotations

M — Message

Trimester-specific, budget-anchored nutrition advice using local staples only

A — Audience

Rural pregnant woman, low-income, no refrigeration, Kakamega County

P — Purpose

Increase safe nutrient intake using accessible, affordable local foods

Key Improvement: Eliminates hallucination of unavailable foods by constraining the AI's food vocabulary to the local ecosystem — directly addressing AfyaTech's documented Kakamega failure where kale was recommended in a county where it is not sold.
Prompt B — Clinic Scheduling
Original AIM MAP
"When should I go to the clinic?"
You are a community health scheduling assistant for rural Western Kenya. You know that 70% of users live more than 5 km from the nearest facility and rely on CHWs who visit on fixed days, typically Tuesday and Friday. A woman is 28 weeks pregnant and has missed her last two ANC visits. Transport costs KES 80 per trip. Based on her gestational age, CHW visit days, and transport costs, help her plan her next clinic visit. Include the best day to go, what documents to bring, and whether to wait for the CHW or travel directly. Do not suggest a specialist hospital unless the CHW recommends it.
